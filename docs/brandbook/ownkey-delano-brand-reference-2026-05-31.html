01Identity

What ownkey is

Ownkey is a keyboard you actually own. It brings modern AI input — voice-to-text and smart assistance — to Android without sending your words to someone else's servers. You bring your own API key; your keystrokes stay yours.

own + monkey = ownkey
// a play on "monkey" — clever, curious — and bringing your "own (api) key"

The tension we hold

Useful AI usually means giving up your data. Ownkey refuses the trade-off: full-featured input, zero data harvesting. The brand should always feel like both — powerful and protective.

Who it's for

People who type a lot and care where their words go: developers, privacy-minded power users, and anyone who'd rather plug in their own key than rent someone else's surveillance.

How it should feel

Like a quality mechanical keycap — tactile, deliberate, built to last. Dark, confident, a little playful. Never corporate, never cute for its own sake.

The promise

Private by default. No data collection. Open source you can read. The monkey is friendly; the privacy is serious.

05Motif

The voice waveform

The orange waveform is the brand's living element. It's the monkey's muzzle in the logo, the listening state in the app, and a graphic device on its own. Vertical rounded bars, varying heights, amber-to-orange gradient.

Rules of use

· Always rounded caps, even spacing, amber→orange vertical gradient.

· Reserve it for voice and AI moments. It signals "ownkey is listening."

· Animate by height only. Never tilt, never rainbow, never outline.

06Voice & Tone

How ownkey talks

Direct, dry, concrete. Confident about privacy, light about everything else. We explain like a smart friend, not a security vendor. The monkey has a personality — we let it show, but we never get cute about your data.
